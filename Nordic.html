What you will learn

The Nordic Data Science is an annual event bringing together the Data Science Community in the Nordics to share ideas, and discuss ways to harness the full potential of Data Science and Machine Learning. The agenda is suited to guide you through the process of extracting knowledge from data by using the latest methodologies, tools and algorithms.
With domestic and international speakers on stage, workshops, interactive panel discussion and plenty of learning and networking activities in the exhibition area, the Nordic Data Science Summit is the place to be for all professionals and organisations in utilizing data science, machine and deep learning to innovate and improve their business.


Areas covered
  • - Business practical case studies on Data Science in an Enterprise
  • - How to handle Real-Time and Streaming Data?
  • - Trends and upcoming Data Science Technologies
  • - Scaling Innovation with Online Experimentation and A/B Testing
  • - Leveraging Machine Learning for gaining Competitive Advantage
  • - Cutting the edge: an insight into deep learning architecture
  • - Why it is worth getting started with Spark
  • - Developing Algorithms and Leveraging AI to Drive Profit, CX and Growth
  • - How to establish an agile and powerful data science environment using the latest in open source tools?

The Nordic Data Science Summit at glance

2 Stages: ● Data Science Stage
Focusing on the organisational and business output of Data Science.
● Machine Learning and AI Stage
Focusing on the technical side of Data Science, Machine Learning, Deep Learning and AI

Domestic and International speakers. All presentations will last 30 minutes, and will be held in English.

Panels: On recomendation of last year's delegates, on this 4 workshops. edition we are adding mixed panels composed by practitioners and experts. By mixing the panel, our intent is to create a good discussion and a contrast between what is possible and what is actually done in organisations today. There will be four panels in total all on different topics and each one addressing a particular part of the summit theme.

Peer to Peer Meetings: To enable more insightful knowledge sharing between peers and provide bigger event experience, delegates this year will have the opportunity to schedule meetings and share contacts via the official event application.

Pre-event Workshops: This year we are kicking off the conference with 4 interactive workshops. Two on Data Science organisation and strategy, and the second two on more technical topics. The duration of the workshops is 2 hours and will be led by some of the most respected experts in this area. As they are going in parallel, you can attend only 2 of the 4 workshops.

Code Katas room: Fashionably organized in US, Code Katas has been a, educational and enlighten exercise for few years. This year we are imbedding the concept in the summit. All Katas are 10 minutes. The task is to get a functional code that coveys a theme. The theme is decided by the performer and reviewed by the advisory board. The schedule is open and due to application directly on the website or by email to goran.cvetanovski@hyperight.com. The application is open to everyone attending the summit.

Demo room: On the recommendation of last year’s delegates, on this edition, we have dedicated a room where leading technology and service providers in the area can demo their latest products and services. All demos are 30 minutes, go in parallel with the agenda, and will be transparently listed on the event webpage, in the final agenda and the event app.

FROM DATA FIRST TO AI FIRST

Welcome to the second edition of Nordic Data Science and Machine Learning summit. We have some exciting changes this year, and we hardly wait to host the event at 7A Odenplan in Stockholm this October.

Concept wise, one of the bigger changes is that we have added one more day to the event focusing on workshops and evening networking program. During the main day, we are keeping the two stages model, but in addition, we have added two extra small rooms for Code Katas and Demos, as well as bigger lounge area which will serve as place for spontaneous and unformal meetings. The main thought with the agenda was to maintain the focus on network knowledge sharing and put you as a delegate in the driver seat. We extended the time for panels and networking, and narrowed the agenda to current trends and implementation challenges.

Content wise, together with the Advisory Board we chose this year's tagline to be 'From Data First to AI First'. Not to point on the fact that we are going to be fully focusing on Artificial Intelligence on this year's summit, but to indicate on where are we heading technology and business wise. Better Insights, automation of manual processes, better use of data, have been the constants throughout the rise of technology especially in the last three decades. By unlocking the full power of structured and unstructured data and applying Artificial Intelligence, we can now automate both routine and nonroutine work. This puts a new dimension on today's data science function both from opportunity and challenge point of view.

On this year's Summit the focus will be on the management, business and technical side of Data Science including Machine Learning, Deep Learning and Artificial Intelligence We tried to balance the time dedicated to each topic and get good practical case studies to address these topics.

The landscape is changing and the Data Science function is maturing. As modern data strategy, digitalization, the increasing demand of ROI from analytics and data science projects are becoming key discussions in management meetings, the data science function need to take the lead in this data driven economy.

Bring your team. We have a lot to discuss.
